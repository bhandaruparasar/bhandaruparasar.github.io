RESEARCH EXPERIENCE

Delicate semimetals

Supervisor: Prof. Vijay B. Shenoy

  • Constructed a novel class of semimetals, Delicate semimetals, protected by unstable homotopies.
  • Demonstrated this with a two-band nodal-line semimetal in four dimensions, carrying a Hopf flux.
  • Studied their remarkable Fermi-arc and drumhead surface states and generalized this construction using a three- dimensional example in the symmetry class AIII.
Fermions coupled to \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) gauge fields

Supervisor: Prof. Vijay B. Shenoy

  • Studied fermions coupled to \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) gauge fields and showed that modulation of fermion hopping realizes many Obstructed Atomic Insulators (OAIs).
  • Analyzed the stability of these phases to quantum fluctuations using Mean field and Renormalization techniques.
  • Found a rich phase diagram with many superfluids, and their evolution from the BEC regime to the BCS regime occurs via a sequence of first order transitions.
